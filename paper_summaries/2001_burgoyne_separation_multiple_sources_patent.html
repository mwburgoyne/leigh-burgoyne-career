Context & Background

This patent (US 6,291,179 B1) represents a significant departure from the seven core FTA family patents. While those addressed DNA storage and preservation, this tackles a different challenge: the separation of genetic material from mixed-source samples on a solid medium. This is a wholly new application (not a continuation), addressing one of the most persistent challenges in forensic genetics — separating DNA from multiple contributors in mixed stains. The assignee is Whatman PLC.

Key Claims & Innovations

  • Claim 1 — Core separation method: Separating selected genetic material from a crude sample on a solid medium using: (1) a cleaning solution of water-miscible solvent at high pH (9.0–13.0) that destroys RNA; (2) an eluting solution; (3) collecting the selected genetic material in the eluate.
  • Claims 2–5: Downstream analysis by PCR, RFLP, or in situ analysis of material remaining on the medium.
  • Claims 6–13: Solid medium composition with protein denaturing agent and optional free radical trap — the FTA chemistry.
  • Claim 14: Explicitly covers multi-source samples.
  • Claims 15–19: Kit claims with solid medium, alkaline cleaning solution, and saline eluting solution.
  • Claim 20: Specific method for separating plasmid DNA from bacterial DNA.

Significance & Contribution

Novel separation chemistry: A three-step process: (1) PAA cleaning (30–90% ethanol with NaOH at pH 9–13) removes proteins, destroys RNA, and cleans the matrix; (2) alcohol rinse; (3) mild saline elution selectively removes smaller DNA while larger genomic DNA remains entangled in the cellulose matrix. This exploits size-dependent DNA retention physics.

Platform extension: Transforms FTA from passive storage to an active sample preparation platform with built-in separation capability. Four worked examples demonstrate plasmid elution, centrifugal stripping, blood DNA recovery, and autoclave-assisted elution.
