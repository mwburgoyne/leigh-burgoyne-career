Context & Background

This 2006 patent application publication is a Continuation-in-Part (CIP) of the 2004 SMIPS patent application (US 2004/0175715 A1). While the parent established the foundational SMIPS (Structurally Mediated Interprimer Selectivity) concept and its basic applications, this CIP extends the intellectual property with substantially expanded claims, additional experimental data, and a refined theoretical framework. Filed October 2005, this version reflects Burgoyne’s continued development of SMIPS technology, moving from proof-of-concept toward a complete analytical methodology with formalised primer equivalence theory and DNA array-based cross-reaction quantification.

Key Claims & Innovations

  • Formalised primer equivalence framework (new): Rigorous definitions of “equivalent primers” (≥5% cross-reaction homology from complex templates), “non-equivalent cross-reactions,” and “SMIPS cross-reactions” versus “simple-sequence cross-reactions.” Absent from the 2004 parent.
  • DNA array cross-reaction analysis (Example 6 — new): Quantitative comparison of SMIPS products from different soil samples immobilised on DNA arrays, probed with fluorescently labelled products. Showed ~20% sequence sharing between soils versus near-zero cross-reaction with human DNA.
  • Expanded sequence listing (56 sequences): Substantial expansion of the primer toolkit with variants of seq005, including equivalents and non-equivalents for systematic comparison.
  • Broadened claims 8–26 (new): Profiling methods, sequencing with 3’-extended primers (≥16 variants), software alignment algorithms, environmental applications, automated robotic devices, kits, and primer design computer programs.
  • Four structural formats (Figure 7): Formally diagrammed SMIPS amplification product folding: Format 1 (open-ended, favoured), Format 2 (CFS, blocked), Format 3 (hairpin loop, long inserts), Format 4 (SLS stem-loop, blocked).
  • Core retained: Fundamental SMIPS methodology, high-C/low-G primer design, pathogen detection examples (HCV, HIV, CVB-4), DNA damage monitoring, and soil profiling from the parent application.

Significance & Contribution

This CIP represents Burgoyne’s maturation of SMIPS from an inventive concept into a complete analytical platform. The most significant advance is the formalisation of primer equivalence theory and the use of DNA arrays for quantitative cross-reaction measurement. Where the 2004 parent demonstrated that SMIPS could produce distinctive profiles, this 2006 CIP showed how to systematically compare those profiles and extract quantitative similarity measures between complex nucleic acid populations. The expanded 56-primer sequence listing transformed SMIPS from a single-primer technique into a configurable toolkit. The broadened claims — particularly those covering sequencing, software algorithms, and automated devices — show Burgoyne anticipating integration of SMIPS into high-throughput genomic pipelines. The soil and environmental applications, now backed by array-based quantitative data, foreshadowed the metagenomics revolution.
