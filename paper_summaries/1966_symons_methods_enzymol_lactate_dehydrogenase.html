L-Lactate (Cytochrome) Dehydrogenase (Crystalline, Yeast)

Authors: R. H. Symons, L. A. Burgoyne

Journal: Methods in Enzymology, 9, 314–321

Year: 1966

DOI: 10.1016/S0076-6879(66)09039-4

Citations: Likely high (Methods in Enzymology chapters serve as standard reference protocols and accumulate citations over decades)

Methods Enzyme Purification Cytochrome b2 Yeast Reference Protocol

Context & Background

Methods in Enzymology is one of the most widely referenced series in biochemistry, providing definitive protocols for enzyme preparation. This chapter established the standard preparation procedure for crystalline yeast cytochrome b2, incorporating the modifications developed by Symons and Burgoyne at Adelaide. It represents the codification of their collective work on this enzyme system into the canonical reference form.

Key Content

  • Complete purification protocol: Detailed 7-step procedure from dried yeast to three-times-crystallised enzyme, including extraction with butanol-lactate, four sequential acetone fractionations, and crystallisation by dialysis. Yields 25–50 mg of purified enzyme (specific activity ~140 units/mg) from 1 kg of dried yeast.
  • Assay methods: Ferricyanide reduction assay at 420 nm; also applicable to whole yeast using butanol permeabilisation.
  • DNA-free enzyme preparation: Method for preparing Type II (DNA-free) enzyme via DEAE-cellulose chromatography, yielding hexagonal bipyramid crystals distinct from the square plate Type I crystals.
  • Oxidised enzyme preparation: Sephadex G-75 procedure for removing lactate substrate to obtain oxidised cytochrome b2.
  • Physical and spectral properties: Detailed reference data including molecular weight (80,000 per protoheme), sedimentation coefficients, absorption spectra, substrate specificity, and inhibitor profiles.
  • Crystallisation artefact noted: Explicitly states that the DNA component is "probably an artifact of the crystallization procedure," citing the companion research papers.

Significance & Contribution

Scientific contribution: As a Methods in Enzymology chapter, this became the standard reference protocol for anyone working with yeast cytochrome b2. These chapters are the "gold standard" for enzyme preparation and are among the most cited items in biochemistry.

Career significance: Being invited to contribute a Methods in Enzymology chapter as a junior researcher (Burgoyne was still early career) indicates the quality and impact of the Adelaide cytochrome b2 work. It also demonstrates practical experimental skill, a characteristic that would define Burgoyne's career through to the development of FTA cards.

Knowledge Built Upon

  • Appleby & Morton (1959): Original crystallisation procedure
  • Morton & Shepley (1963): Type II enzyme and related procedures
  • Nicholls, Atkinson, Burgoyne & Symons (1966): Modification during crystallisation
  • Burgoyne & Symons (1966): DNA component artefact demonstration

What It Contributed To

  • Standard reference for all subsequent work on yeast cytochrome b2
  • Widely cited in the flavoprotein and yeast enzyme literature for decades
