Forensic Analysis of Soils Using Single Arbitrarily Primed Amplification and High Throughput Sequencing

Authors: Anastasia S. Khodakova, Leigh Burgoyne, Damien Abarno, Adrian Linacre

Journal: Forensic Science International: Genetics Supplement Series, Vol. 4, e39–e40

Year: 2013

DOI: 10.1016/j.fsigss.2013.10.019

Citations: ~6 (OpenAlex)

Forensic Soil Analysis Soil Metagenomics High Throughput Sequencing Arbitrarily Primed PCR Ion PGM MG-RAST

Context & Background

By 2013, Burgoyne’s long-standing interest in forensic soil DNA profiling — first demonstrated in his 2004 SMIPS patent (US 2004/0175715 A1) — was converging with the revolution in next-generation sequencing. Traditional DNA fingerprinting of soils by fragment-length variation had proven unreliable due to poor reproducibility. The advent of high throughput sequencing platforms, particularly the Ion PGM benchtop sequencer, offered a path to sequence-level characterisation of complex soil microbial communities. This conference paper, presented at the 25th Congress of the International Society for Forensic Genetics, represents the first peer-reviewed demonstration from Burgoyne’s group combining arbitrarily primed PCR with HTS for forensic soil discrimination.

Key Findings

  • Seq5 primer for HTS: The same single arbitrary primer (5’-GGAGGTGGTGTTCGAGGG-3’) from Burgoyne’s 2004 SMIPS patent was used for sequence-independent amplification and selective pre-enrichment of metagenomic DNA from three Adelaide soil sites located 3–4 km apart.
  • Ion PGM sequencing: Generated 449,262 reads (average length 189 ± 49 bp), with ~30% annotated against the SEED protein database via MG-RAST.
  • Functional discrimination: Statistically significant differences (p < 0.05) between sites in carbohydrates, lipid metabolism, iron acquisition, RNA metabolism, stress response, membrane transport, cell wall/capsule, and phosphorus metabolism.
  • Taxonomic discrimination: Distinct phylum-level signatures: Acidobacteria/Planctomicetes/Firmicutes (site A); Actinobacteria/Bacteroidetes/Chloroflexi/Verrucomicrobia (site B); Proteobacteria/Deinococcus-Thermus (site C).
  • Validation: Dominant taxa consistent with gold-standard 16S rRNA and shotgun sequencing methods.

Significance & Contribution

This paper is a proof-of-concept that Burgoyne’s single-primer arbitrarily primed PCR — originally developed as a gel-based fingerprinting tool — could serve as a viable front-end for high throughput metagenomic sequencing in forensic applications. By using a single arbitrary primer for selective pre-enrichment, the method reduced the complexity and cost of metagenomic library preparation while generating data rich enough for both taxonomic and functional community profiling. The fact that the primer (Seq5) is the same one described in Burgoyne’s 2004 patent demonstrates direct continuity from invention to empirical validation. It established the methodological foundation for the more comprehensive Khodakova et al. (2014) PLoS ONE paper.
