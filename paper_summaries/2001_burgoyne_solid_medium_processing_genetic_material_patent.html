Context & Background

This patent (US 6,322,983 B1) is the eighth US patent in the FTA card family, a further continuation from the US 5,985,327 chain. The specification and examples are identical to all prior family members. What distinguishes this patent is its use of “consisting of” — closed transitional language that limits the invention to exactly the recited elements. All seven prior FTA patents used “comprising” (open) or means-plus-function formulations.

Key Claims & Innovations

  • Claim 1: Solid medium consisting of a solid matrix and a composition consisting of a protein denaturing agent and an inhibitor of an action of a nuclease — the simplest two-component closed claim in the family.
  • Claim 2: Method consisting essentially of applying genetic material to such a medium and processing the sample.
  • Claim 3: Method with composition comprising a protein denaturing agent, chelating agent, and uric acid.
  • Claim 4: Solid medium consisting of a solid matrix with protein denaturing agent, “compound that binds ions which promote degradation of DNA,” and a buffer.

Significance & Contribution

First use of closed “consisting of” claim language in the FTA estate, complementing open claims. Introduction of “inhibitor of an action of a nuclease” — broader than “chelating agent” alone. Title broadened from “Storage” to “Processing” of genetic material. Together, the eight core FTA continuations now span every major claim strategy.

Knowledge Built Upon

What It Contributed To
