Random Whole Metagenomic Sequencing for Forensic Discrimination of Soils

Authors: Anastasia S. Khodakova, Renee J. Smith, Leigh Burgoyne, Damien Abarno, Adrian Linacre

Journal: PLoS ONE, 9(8): e104996

Year: 2014

DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0104996

Citations: ~49 (OpenAlex)

Forensic Soil Analysis Soil Metagenomics Arbitrarily Primed PCR Shotgun Sequencing Whole Genome Amplification Ion Torrent PGM MG-RAST Bray-Curtis Similarity CAP Ordination

Context & Background

By 2014, the proof-of-concept conference paper by Khodakova, Burgoyne, Abarno & Linacre (2013) had demonstrated that Burgoyne’s Seq5 single arbitrary primer could serve as a front-end for high-throughput soil metagenomic sequencing. This full-length PLoS ONE paper expanded the investigation into a rigorous head-to-head comparison of three random whole-metagenome sequencing approaches — shotgun, whole genome amplification (WGA), and arbitrarily primed PCR (AP-PCR) — for forensic discrimination of visually similar urban soils. Funded by the Attorney General’s Office of South Australia, the study addressed a critical gap: there was no validated method for comparing the DNA content of soils in forensic casework.

Key Findings

  • Three methods compared: Shotgun, WGA (Phi29 polymerase), and AP-PCR sequencing of triplicate soil samples from two Adelaide parklands ~3 km apart, using Ion Torrent PGM.
  • Shotgun and WGA failed: Both standard metagenomic approaches generated highly similar profiles across locations, with frequent misclassification in cross-validation of CAP models.
  • AP-PCR succeeded: The single arbitrary primer approach achieved 100% correct site-specific classification from class level upward across all taxonomic and metabolic hierarchies.
  • Thorough statistical validation: CLUSTER, SIMPROF, NMDS, and CAP analyses at all MG-RAST taxonomic and metabolic hierarchies confirmed AP-PCR superiority.
  • Dominant taxa: Proteobacteria, Actinobacteria, and Bacteroidetes comprised ~70% of annotated reads across all methods.
  • Subsampled datasets confirmed: RELATE analysis showed strong Spearman rank correlations (r > 0.9) between full and subsampled (49,000 reads) datasets.

Significance & Contribution

This paper is the definitive validation of Burgoyne’s arbitrarily primed PCR approach for forensic soil metagenomics, demonstrating for the first time that AP-PCR outperforms both shotgun and WGA-based sequencing for discriminating visually similar urban soils. The critical insight is that shotgun and WGA methods are dominated by highly similar abundant taxa, masking site-specific differences, while the single arbitrary primer acts as a pre-enrichment filter that targets both dominant and rare organisms equally. With ~49 citations, this is the most-cited paper from Burgoyne’s forensic soil metagenomics programme, representing the culmination of a research arc stretching from the 2004 SMIPS patent through the 2012 arbitrary primer validation to this systematic comparison.

What It Contributed To

  • Established AP-PCR + HTS as the leading candidate method for forensic soil discrimination
  • Roadmap for forensic validation: expanded geographic sampling, temporal variation studies, blind trials
  • Open-access metagenomic datasets deposited on MG-RAST for community use
  • Khodakova, Burgoyne, Abarno & Linacre (2015) — temporal stability study demonstrating AP-PCR soil profiles are stable across seasons
  • Burgoyne, Koh & Catcheside (2017, Methods in Molecular Biology) — formalised AP-PCR protocols for the wider community
  • Burgoyne, Koh & Catcheside (2017, FSIGSS) — extended the approach to vehicle-surface DNA forensics
  • Broader adoption of metagenomics-based forensic evidence frameworks
